Stay prepared

Safety Tips

Small habits that make a real difference. Read through and share them with your friends and neighbours.

Avoid isolated places at night

Stick to well-lit, populated routes after dark, even if it takes a little longer to get where you're going.

Walk with friends

There's safety in numbers. Arrange to move around campus with a friend, especially in the evenings.

Report suspicious activity immediately

Don't wait — the sooner a report reaches security, the faster they can respond and prevent escalation.

Save emergency contact numbers

Keep campus security, police and medical emergency numbers saved and easy to reach on your phone.

Keep valuables secure

Avoid displaying phones, laptops or cash openly, and always lock your room and hostel doors.

Stay alert in crowded places

Crowds can mask pickpocketing and harassment — keep your bag close and stay aware of your surroundings.

Together we're safer

Every report you file helps protect someone else

Safety on campus is a shared responsibility. When you speak up, you give security teams the visibility they need to keep everyone — students, staff and residents — protected.
